Context

LM‐Sounds a DJ from Herentals wanted a new and updated website. The previous one was made in wix and really needed to updated. Lander, a friend of mine, is the idea behind LM‐Sounds. The group plays at weddings, business and birthday parties.

When we were chatting, we suddenly came to the subject websites. After a while Lander said he needed a new website. Because I was immediately interested I started to ask questions and before we knew he had explained me the whole idea of their site. A few days later I had made a sketch and sent it to him to check and get feedback.

The next step was developing the website with my other Maxim. First thing was getting the basics, what really had to be integrated into the website and what could be nice to have but not necessary. We always tried to keep Lander informed on our progress.

Conclusion

What I learned is: communicating to your customer (in this case a friend). Lander did not have much knowledge so if you need to explain something, you had to be sure that he would understand it. Also keeping the client up to date is nice. That way he knows if you are making progress and just have almost instant feedback.

While creating the website I mostly focused on the content and what elements should be on the website or not. Maxim focused on the styling of the site. Of course Lander had the final word, and every big decision was only made when he agreed with it.

I really enjoyed this project, not only to create a website but also to help a friend and support him in the long run. We also keep the website up to date and try to make sure the hosting is always online.
